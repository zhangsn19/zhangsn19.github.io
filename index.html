About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Tsinghua University. My research centers on the privacy and security aspects of Human-AI Interaction. My work has been published in premier Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) venues, such as CHI, CSCW, and IMWUT, in addition to top-tier conferences in security (CCS) and artificial intelligence (IJCAI). In recognition of my contributions, my research received a Best Paper Candidate Award at IJCAI'25 DDL workshop. I actively contribute to the academic community by serving as a Session Chair for CHI'25 and as a reviewer for conferences including CSCW, Ubicomp, and IUI. Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies in Computer Science at Tsinghua University, advised by Professor Yuanchun Shi and Professor Xin Yi. I have also undertaken research internships at Stanford University with Professor James A. Landay, the University of Washington with Professor Anind K. Dey and Professor Jennifer Mankoff, and the PLUS Lab at UCLA under the supervision of Professor Nanyun Peng.

Looking forward to a visiting position or collaborations from all around the world! I currently have collaborations with researchers from Stanford University, University of Washington, University of Melbourne, University of Luxembourg, University of New South Wales, Aalto University, Beihang University, Southeast University, etc.
